Trouble has been Brewing in the Agricultural Community

The agricultural community has seen a steady decline in farms since the early 20th century resulting in a displacement of the farming community. There has been a steady decline in the debt amongst the farming community that has been building for decades and becoming increasing worse each year.

"From 1993 to 2017, real (inflation-adjusted) farm debt increased by 87 percent, or 4 percent per year on average. ERS forecasts farm debt to increase 2 percent in both 2018 and 2019." -Farm Policy News

The Effects of the Agricultural Crisis

"Most farmers receive off-farm income, but small-scale operators depend on it." - USDA

"After peaking at 6.8 million farms in 1935, the number of U.S. farms fell sharply until leveling off in the early 1970s. Falling farm numbers during this period reflected growing productivity in agriculture and increased nonfarm employment opportunities." - USDA

Purpose of this Website:

This is a running template for a centralized platform for the agricultural community to share their information on their excess of goods online with individual people. This application is a basic start-up template to connect consumers to farmers and "purchase" particular goods from farmers including donations for labor and supplies. This gives monetary relief to farmers who are suffering from bankruptcy or who cannot afford to continue producing while enabling a supply flow to foodbanks or food pantries.

Since this is a demo project, the entirety of the application is not connected to any paypal or credit card routes as this would require extra measures in security that cannot be done without proper training. However, the goal is to evenutally lead up to handing this service to a donation center for further monitering and ability to write tax deductible funds as more of an incentive to give to the agricultural community.

Using this website would begin a communication and a slow build towards transparency in the agricultural community and the consumers in a farm to table fashion.
